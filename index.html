Alpha

Cronyx's newest game has been in development since May of 2018. It is a pseudo-sokoban style puzzle game taking inspiration from Fez and Bloxorz. In our game, you will attempt to rotate various 3-dimensional blocks into a particular orientation by manipulating the game's world in two dimensions. The games 3-dimensional geometry is flattened into 2-dimensions, allowing movement that seems to defy reason. This mechanic will force you to reimagine the way you move and explore the space around you.

With 14 worlds and 72 levels to explore, the game is not for the faint of heart. Fortunately for you, much of the development gruntwork has already been completed. But in order to fine-tune our work, we would like to ask a favor of you: play it! We're asking all members of the community to join in Cronyx's open-alpha from September 11th to September 23rd! At the bottom of this page you'll find a download of the game's beta version. We'd request that you fill out a feedback form after playing to give you a chance to share any feedback/criticisms. While the game is still in a rough state, we'd love to hear what you think!

Cronyx plans to submit its game for the 2020 Independent Games Festival at the GDC in San Francisco, California. For any additional information, email us at info@cronyxgames.com. See you on the flip side!

about

Cronyx is an independent-game studio based in Albuquerque, New Mexico and headed by Jacob Roberts-Baca. Founded in 2019 by Samuel and Jacob Roberts-Baca, we strive to bring out the best that game development can offer through simplicity, creativity and experimentation.
